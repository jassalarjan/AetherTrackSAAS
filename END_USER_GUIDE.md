# AetherTrackSAAS End-User Guide

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 2026

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document provides comprehensive guidance for end users of the AetherTrackSAAS platform. It explains how to use the platform's core features to manage tasks, projects, teams, and HR operations effectively.

This guide serves as your primary reference for understanding platform functionality. Each section explains both the purpose of each feature and the step-by-step procedures for using it. Refer to this document whenever you need clarification on platform operations.

---

## 2. Who Should Read This Document

This guide is designed for the following users:

- **Team Members:** Individuals who need to manage their daily tasks, track time, and collaborate with teammates.
- **Team Leads:** Users responsible for overseeing team performance, managing team tasks, and coordinating project work.
- **Managers:** Professionals who need visibility into team activities, project progress, and organizational metrics.
- **HR Staff:** Personnel responsible for managing attendance, leave requests, holidays, and team composition.
- **Workspace Administrators:** Users who manage workspace settings, user access, and platform configuration within their organization.

You do not need technical expertise to use this guide. All explanations assume no prior experience with the platform.

## 3. High-Level Overview

AetherTrackSAAS is an integrated platform that combines four essential business functions into a unified experience:

### 3.1 Task and Project Management

The platform provides multiple ways to organize and track work. You can create individual tasks, organize them into projects, and visualize progress using Kanban boards or Gantt charts. Teams using agile methodologies can create sprints to manage iterative work cycles. This flexibility allows you to choose the method that best fits your workflow.

### 3.2 HR Operations

AetherTrack simplifies HR processes by centralizing attendance tracking, leave management, and holiday scheduling. Employees can clock in and out, request time off, and view approved holidays. Managers and HR staff can review requests, approve or decline leaves, and maintain accurate attendance records.

### 3.3 Team Collaboration

The platform facilitates real-time collaboration through instant notifications and activity updates. You receive alerts when tasks are assigned to you, when status changes occur, or when colleagues mention you in comments. This ensures everyone stays informed without needing to constantly check the platform manually.

### 3.4 Analytics and Reporting

AetherTrack includes analytics tools that transform raw data into visual reports. These reports help you understand team productivity, project progress, leave patterns, and other key metrics. You can access pre-built dashboards or generate custom reports based on your needs.

## 4. Getting Started

This section explains how to access the platform and understand your initial view.

### 4.1 Logging In

To access AetherTrack, you need valid credentials provided by your workspace administrator. Open your web browser and navigate to the platform URL provided by your organization. Enter your email address and password in the login fields, then click the Sign In button.

If you forget your password, use the "Forgot Password" link on the login page to reset it. The system will send a reset link to your registered email address.

Upon successful login, you arrive at your dashboard, which serves as your central hub for all platform activities.

### 4.2 Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard displays an aggregated view of items requiring your attention. The exact content depends on your role and permissions, but typically includes Tasks:** Tasks:

- **Pending assigned to you that are not yet completed.
- **Recent Activity:** A chronological list of recent changes relevant to you.
- **Upcoming Deadlines:** Tasks with approaching due dates.
- **Quick Actions:** Buttons for common operations like creating a new task or requesting leave.
- **Notifications Panel:** A summary of unread notifications.

The dashboard refreshes automatically to show real-time updates. You can customize your dashboard layout if your role permits.

### 4.3 Navigation

The main navigation menu appears on the left side of the screen. Common menu items include:

- **Dashboard:** Your home screen with overview information.
- **Tasks:** Access the task management area.
- **Projects:** View and manage projects.
- **Teams:** See team structures and memberships.
- **HR:** Access attendance, leave, and holiday features.
- **Analytics:** View reports and visualizations.
- **Settings:** Manage your personal preferences and account settings.

Click any menu item to navigate to that section. The current section is highlighted in the navigation menu.

### 4.4 Profile and Preferences

Click your profile icon in the top-right corner to access personal settings. From here you can:

- Update your display name and picture
- Change your password
- Configure notification preferences
- Set profile your timezone and language preferences
- View your assigned roles and permissions

## 5. Task Management

Tasks are the fundamental units of work in AetherTrack. Understanding how to create, manage, and track tasks is essential for using the platform effectively.

### 5.1 Creating Tasks

To create a new task, navigate to the Tasks section and click the "New Task" or "+" button. Complete the following required fields:

- **Title:** A brief, descriptive name for the task.
- **Description:** Detailed information about what the task involves.
- **Assignee:** The team member responsible for completing the task.
- **Due Date:** The deadline for task completion.
- **Priority:** The urgency level (Low, Medium, High, or Critical).

You can also specify additional details such as:

- Tags or labels for categorization
- Attachments or relevant files
- Subtasks for breaking down complex work
- Estimated hours for planning purposes

After completing the task details, click "Save" or "Create" to add the task to the system.

### 5.2 Managing Tasks

Once created, tasks can be modified at any time. Open a task to view its details, then use the edit function to change any field. You can update the status, reassign the task, modify the due date, or add comments as work progresses.

Tasks progress through defined statuses such as "To Do," "In Progress," "Review," and "Complete." Updating the status reflects the current state of work.

### 5.3 Kanban Board

The Kanban board provides a visual interface for managing tasks. Tasks appear as cards organized into columns representing different stages of work. You can drag and drop cards between columns to update status quickly.

To access the Kanban view, navigate to the Tasks section and select the Kanban option. The default columns typically include "To Do," "In Progress," "Done," but administrators can customize column names to match your workflow.

Using the Kanban board helps teams visualize work in progress, identify bottlenecks, and maintain a steady flow of tasks through completion.

### 5.4 Filtering and Searching

When you have many tasks, use filters to find specific items quickly. Filter options include:

- Assignee: Find tasks assigned to a specific person
- Status: Show tasks in a particular stage
- Priority: Display tasks by urgency level
- Due Date: Show tasks due today, this week, or within a custom range
- Tags: Find tasks with specific labels

The search function allows you to locate tasks by entering keywords from the title or description.

### 5.5 Task Comments and Collaboration

Each task includes a comments section where team members can discuss work. Use comments to:

- Ask questions about task requirements
- Provide updates on progress
- Share relevant files or links
- Mention colleagues using @username to notify them

Comments create a permanent record of discussions, ensuring everyone stays informed about task-related conversations.

## 6. Team Management

Teams organize users into logical groups for collaboration and management purposes. Understanding team structures helps you find the right people and understand reporting relationships.

### 6.1 Viewing Teams

Navigate to the Teams section to see all teams in your workspace. The team list displays:

- Team name
- Team lead
- Number of members
- Associated projects

Click on any team to view its details, including the member list and team-specific settings.

### 6.2 Team Members

Each team consists of members who collaborate on shared work. As a team member, you can view other members, see their roles, and understand team composition.

Team leads and administrators can add or remove members, assign roles, and manage team settings.

### 6.3 Team Leads

Team leads oversee specific teams and have additional responsibilities including:

- Creating and assigning tasks to team members
- Monitoring team progress and performance
- Reviewing and approving leave requests (for HR-related features)
- Coordinating team activities and meetings

If you are a team lead, your dashboard may show team-specific metrics and management options.

### 6.4 Finding Colleagues

Use the user directory or team pages to find colleagues. Each user profile contains contact information, assigned teams, current projects, and reporting relationships. This helps you identify the right person to contact for specific questions or approvals.

## 7. Project Management

Projects organize related tasks into cohesive units of work. Using projects helps teams track progress toward larger goals and maintain context for interconnected tasks.

### 7.1 Creating Projects

To create a new project, navigate to the Projects section and click "New Project." Provide the following information:

- **Project Name:** A clear, descriptive title
- **Description:** An overview of the project's purpose and scope
- **Start Date:** When project work begins
- **Target End Date:** The planned completion date
- **Team:** The team responsible for the project

You can also configure settings such as visibility (who can view the project), notifications, and access permissions.

### 7.2 Project Views

AetherTrack provides multiple ways to view project information:

- **List View:** Displays tasks in a tabular format with sortable columns.
- **Kanban View:** Shows tasks as cards organized by status.
- **Gantt View:** Presents a timeline chart showing task durations and dependencies.

Switch between views using the options available in the project section. Choose the view that best supports your planning or review needs.

### 7.3 Project Progress

Each project includes progress indicators showing how much work is complete. These indicators update automatically as tasks move to completed statuses. You can view:

- Percentage of tasks completed
- Tasks due and overdue
- Time spent versus estimates
- Team member workload distribution

### 7.4 Sprints

For teams using agile methodologies, sprints organize work into time-boxed iterations. To use sprints:

1. Create a sprint within a project, specifying the sprint duration (typically 1-4 weeks).
2. Add tasks to the sprint from the project backlog.
3. Track sprint progress using the sprint board.
4. Review completed work when the sprint ends.

Sprints help teams maintain focus, deliver value incrementally, and continuously improve their processes.

## 8. HR Features

AetherTrack includes tools for managing attendance, leave, and holidays. These features streamline HR operations and ensure accurate records.

### 8.1 Attendance Tracking

The attendance system records when employees clock in and out of work. To record your attendance:

1. Navigate to the HR or Attendance section.
2. Click "Clock In" when starting work.
3. Click "Clock Out" when ending work.

The system records the date and time of each clock action. Your attendance history is stored and used for reporting purposes.

Managers can view attendance records for their team members to monitor punctuality and attendance patterns.

### 8.2 Leave Requests

When you need time off work, submit a leave request through the platform:

1. Navigate to the Leave section.
2. Click "New Leave Request" or "Request Leave."
3. Select the leave type (annual leave, sick leave, personal leave, etc.).
4. Specify the start date and end date.
5. Add a reason or notes if required.
6. Submit the request for approval.

Your request is routed to the appropriate approver (typically your manager or HR staff). You receive notifications when your request is approved or declined.

### 8.3 Viewing Leave Balance

Access your leave balance to see available days for each leave type. The balance display shows:

- Total days allocated for the period
- Days already used
- Days pending approval
- Remaining available days

Check your balance before submitting leave requests to ensure you have sufficient days available.

### 8.4 Holidays

The platform maintains a calendar of approved holidays. Navigate to the Holidays section to view:

- Upcoming holidays with dates
- Holiday names and descriptions
- Whether holidays are mandatory or optional

Holiday information helps you plan leave requests around non-working days.

### 8.5 HR Calendar

The HR calendar provides an integrated view of:

- Approved leave for team members
- Scheduled holidays
- Team events and meetings

Use this calendar to understand team availability and plan accordingly.

## 9. Notifications and Real-Time Updates

AetherTrack keeps you informed about relevant activities through notifications and real-time updates.

### 9.1 Notification Types

You receive notifications for events including:

- **Task Assignments:** When a task is assigned to you
- **Status Changes:** When a task you created or are assigned to changes status
- **Comments:** When someone comments on your tasks or mentions you
- **Due Date Reminders:** When tasks are approaching their deadlines
- **Leave Status:** When your leave request is approved or declined
- **Mention Notifications:** When someone uses @username to reference you

### 9.2 Notification Channels

Notifications appear in multiple places:

- **In-App Notifications:** A notification icon in the header shows unread count. Click to view the full list.
- **Email Notifications:** Selected notifications are sent to your email address.
- **Browser Notifications:** If enabled, the browser shows desktop notifications for important updates.

### 9.3 Configuring Notifications

You can customize which notifications you receive and through which channels:

1. Go to Settings or Profile Preferences.
2. Find the Notifications section.
3. Toggle notifications on or off for specific event types.
4. Select your preferred delivery method (in-app, email, or both).

Adjust these settings to balance staying informed without being overwhelmed by notifications.

## 10. Analytics and Reports

The analytics section provides data-driven insights into various aspects of your organization's operations.

### 10.1 Available Reports

AetherTrack offers multiple visualization types including:

- **Task Analytics:** Completion rates, turnaround times, backlog analysis
- **Project Progress:** Timeline adherence, milestone completion, resource utilization
- **Team Performance:** Workload distribution, productivity metrics, attendance patterns
- **Leave Analytics:** Usage trends, popular leave periods, balance utilization
- **Attendance Reports:** Punctuality statistics, attendance trends, absent days

### 10.2 Accessing Analytics

Navigate to the Analytics section to view available reports. The exact reports visible depend on your role and permissions. Managers and administrators typically have access to broader organizational data, while individual contributors see personal or team-level metrics.

### 10.3 Generating Custom Reports

If pre-built reports do not meet your needs, you may be able to generate custom reports by:

1. Selecting the data you want to analyze
2. Choosing visualization type (chart style)
3. Setting filters for specific time periods, teams, or categories
4. Running the report to generate results

Custom reports can be saved for future access or exported for sharing.

### 10.4 Interpreting Visualizations

Charts and graphs present data in accessible formats:

- **Bar Charts:** Compare values across categories
- **Line Charts:** Show trends over time
- **Pie Charts:** Display proportional distributions
- **Tables:** Provide detailed numerical data

Read chart titles and axis labels carefully to understand what data is being presented. Hover over data points to see specific values.

## 11. Edge Cases, Assumptions, and Constraints

This section addresses specific scenarios and limitations you may encounter while using the platform.

### 11.1 Assumptions

The platform operates with the following assumptions:

- Users have valid credentials provided by their organization
- Each user belongs to at least one workspace
- Tasks and projects are associated with specific workspaces
- Leave balances are managed according to organizational policies
- Time zones are configured correctly for accurate reporting

### 11.2 Common Edge Cases

**Cannot Log In:** Verify your credentials are correct. Contact your administrator if you continue having issues. Your account may be disabled or not yet activated.

**Task Not Visible:** Check that you have the correct workspace selected and appropriate permissions. Tasks may be filtered or belong to a different workspace.

**Leave Request Rejected:** Review the rejection reason provided. Resubmit with adjusted dates or additional justification if needed.

**Notifications Not Received:** Check your notification settings and spam folder. Verify your email address is correct in your profile.

**Report Shows No Data:** Confirm you have the correct date range and filters selected. You may lack permission to view the requested data.

### 11.3 Constraints

Certain limitations apply to platform usage:

- **Community Workspace Limits:** Community workspaces support maximum 10 users, 100 tasks, and 3 teams. Exceeding these limits requires upgrading to a CORE workspace.
- **Role Permissions:** Your access to features depends on your assigned role. Contact your administrator if you need additional permissions.
- **Data Retention:** Organization policies may affect how long historical data is retained. Check with your administrator for specific policies.
- **File Attachments:** File size and type restrictions may apply to attachments. Refer to documentation or administrator for allowed formats.

### 11.4 Support and Assistance

If you encounter issues not covered in this guide:

1. Check the platform's help resources or knowledge base
2. Contact your workspace administrator for role-specific questions
3. Reach out to your manager for workflow-related assistance
4. Submit a support ticket for technical issues
